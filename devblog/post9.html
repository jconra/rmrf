Teach the winners to finish

2026-07-08 · v321–v323 · flag capture is the ONLY win — ruled by the designer, so every broken last mile had to be repaired, not ruled around

Yesterday ended with a ruling: no annihilation victory, no concession rule — a match ends when a flag comes home, full stop. That turned four "exhausted war" stalemates from a rules question into an engineering list. Four seeds where one side had won the war and could not finish it. It turned out to be four different diseases.

4/4
broken finisher chains — all four now end in a captured flag
SEED 207
The unshootable keep

Six firebrats and a valkyrie against a 297hp HQ with zero towers — stuck forever. The probe showed a lurcher parked 33u from the keep, ammo pinned at 68 for 160+ seconds. It wasn't missing; it was never firing. The HQ has been a solid obstacle since v156, which means a line-of-sight ray to its own centre always dies inside the building. No LOS → no fire clearance. The fallback shoots the nearest live wall — all rubble by then. The last fallback needs the unit's nose physically pressed against it — but the standoff logic holds it politely 24u away. Three layers of siege logic, each handing the job to the next, none allowed to take the shot.

CURE: with no wall left, the keep itself becomes the demolish target — square onto the hull and shell it open. Resolved 594s.
SEED 137
Runner-feeding the tower gauntlet

Flag exposed, two towers still live on the approach. Runner dies to an enemy vehicle → the doctrine escalates (clear the interceptor first). Runner dies to towers → "sneak in wide"… and that's the whole plan. A fresh firebrat was rebuilt from scrap and shredded by the same guns every ~30 seconds for 800 seconds, while two jotuns sat in the garage. The commander was rich, patient, and wrong.

"Lost the runner — go quiet, we're sneaking the next one in wide!" ×26
CURE (the designer's own rule: "I would send a stronger unit to deal with the problem"): stealth once — repeat tower-deaths force a timed SIEGE window that fields a real sieger to silence the towers before the next grab. New radio line: "the towers keep shredding our runners — silencing them first."
SEED 193
The win nobody could buy

Flag grabbable, HQ dead, 15 scrap banked, zero firebrats. The emergency build-a-runner logic exists — inside deploy(), which only runs when a unit dies or gets recalled. The stand-in lurcher never died (enemy eliminated) and capture never changes step, so the commander orbited fuel-RTB loops for 800s with the win sitting fully funded in its bank.

CURE: a runner-swap check inside the recall logic — on capture with a grabbable flag and a non-firebrat fielded, if a real runner is in the garage or affordable, force the recall. deploy() then builds and fields it. Resolved 546s: "The flag's sitting right there — pull the lurcher back, we're sending a real runner!"
SEED 137 (again)
The wedge no timer could see

After the doctrine fixes, 137 became a live two-sided race — and then stalled anyway. The tournament named it: blue firebrat 802s @shore · why=wedged on terrain · blk=···. Every stuck reflex was blind to it: the still-timer resets because the hull micro-slides each tick; the wedge-timer needs a feeler pressed on an obstacle (blk=··· — nothing); the nav blacklist keys off the still-timer. A runner ground against a shore slope for 13 minutes while three watchdogs reported all-clear.

CURE: a net-progress watchdog — 10s displacement window; wanted to move most of it but covered <4u → reverse-pivot jolt (alternating sides), blacklist the spot, replan. Fires on outcome, not symptoms. Resolved 1186s.
