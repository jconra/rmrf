№1 — The gambit that deadlocked itself

Three of six matches contained one log line repeated thousands of times: “Break off the grind — pull the lurcher back, Valkyrie’s going around the back!” That’s the stalemate gambit ordering a vehicle swap. The order was re-issued every tick, because two safety rules fought each other:

rival within 46u
ABORT recall
“don’t drive home under fire”
⇄
gambit wants Valkyrie
COMMIT recall
“even with defenders near”

Abort and re-commit alternated forever. The unit received a steering command only every other tick, the stalemate-breaking Valkyrie never launched — and the gambit exists precisely for matches where an enemy is always nearby. One frozen 559-second lurcher duel sat directly on top of this. Fix (v300): a committed recall now drives home even under fire — that is what “commit” means.

№2 — The sieger’s two-spot shuttle

Seed 67’s report was a wall of the same event: a jotun in suppress, goal 75–185u away, shuttling between two spots ~10u apart for the whole match. Two causes:

  • The trek to a siege standoff can be 100u+ around terrain, but suppress travel had no A* — just direct steering and bump-feelers.
  • A recent “if wedged, plant and shoot” fallback would plant even when there was nothing shootable from that spot, silently resetting the stuck timers each cycle.

Fix (v300): suppress now path-follows A* until within 26u of its standoff, and the plant-and-fire fallback requires an actual target (a bearing line on the tower, or a wall in range to flatten). Seed 67 flipped from stalemate to RESOLVED on the re-run.

№3 — Engage flap: the LOS strobe

With the big blockers gone, run 2’s top signal became combat-state thrash: suppress↔engage ×54 in 21s, pursue↔engage ×50 in 36s. In a duel both hulls weave through cover, so line-of-sight blinks constantly — and every blink bounced the whole state ladder. Watch it happen (replay of seed 25’s blue jotun, 20× speed; the dot is the unit, colour = its state):

engage pursue suppress other
state timeline

Fix (v301): engage afterglow — if a unit was fighting and its rival vanished less than 0.9s ago, the brain keeps treating it as visible at its last seen position. Blinks merge into one continuous engagement; the state only changes once the target is genuinely gone. Entry into engage stays instant.
