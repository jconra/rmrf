The panic loop

Sweep 5's top signal: capture firebrats bouncing pursue↔flee ×7–11 under fire, spamming "Taking fire — breaking off toward snatching the flag!". The flee latch looked guilty but was innocent — it already has clean hysteresis (trip at 60u, clear at 100u). The loop was the other half of the cycle: after a successful escape past 100u, the pursuing condition — which unlike runnerFlee and threatened had no runner gate — sent the firebrat chasing the very enemy it had just escaped. Back inside 60u, flee again, repeat until dead.

Fix: a flag runner never pursues sightings. It resumes the mission; the flee latch handles genuine chasers. Sweep 6: the pursue↔flee signature is gone from every seed.

The corner pin

Seed 67's fleeing firebrat pressed itself against the island's hard travel rim at (111,−87) and jolt-stormed there — flee steers pure away-from-threat, and away happened to point off the world. Fix: near the rim, if the escape vector still presses outward, bend it along the tangent (the side already matching the escape) with a slight inward ease — the runner skirts the rim past its pursuer. Sweep 6: zero unstick storms, and seed 67 resolved.
