The war both sides won (and neither finished)

2026-07-07 · seed 25 · stalemate in 10 of 11 sweeps · v314

Seed 25 outlived every fix. After the railgun, the flaps, the panic loops — it kept stalemating, and its reports came back clean: no storms, no wedged units. So this one got a war-correspondent treatment instead of a debugger: read the whole 800 seconds and ask why doesn't anybody win?

The war, in one graph

Each line is the damage a team has done to the enemy base (structure HP remaining). Falling lines = real progress. Watch both lines dive — this is not a passive match:

blue's base (red attacking) red's base (blue attacking) | = a vehicle destroyed

By t≈450: both HQs dead, both flags exposed, red's base stripped to 0 turrets, blue's to 2. Twenty-one vehicles destroyed, kills 9–10. A fair, brutal, fully-functional war that reached "either side can win at any moment"… and then produced nothing for 350 seconds.

The two rings

The dance is two constants fighting:

runnerFlee trips when an enemy is within 60u — and a defender camps the exposed flag, so the fear-ring is centred right on the prize. The final-dash commit (capturing: "beeline it, ignore everything") only engaged within 55u of the flag. The fear ring enclosed the commit ring: every approach hit fear at ~60u out, fled to 100u, returned, hit fear again. The runner physically could not reach the radius where bravery begins. Red's one successful grab happened in a moment the defender drifted wide.

Fix (v314): CAPTURE_COMMIT 55 → 85 — the commit ring now encloses the fear ring. Inside 85u of a grabbable flag, the runner goes deaf to fear and dashes: grab or die trying. Both outcomes resolve — and the counter-play stays with the defender and the intercept, where it belongs, instead of with an endless dance in the 60–100u shell.

⏳ Sweep 12 (v314) is running — verification lands here.

Takeaway

Not every stalemate is a bug in the machinery. This one was a doctrine flaw — caution calibrated for mid-game survival, still in charge during the endgame sprint, with the two radii accidentally inverted. The detectors couldn't see it because nothing repeated in place; it took reading the whole war like a story. Some bugs live in the plot, not the sentences.
