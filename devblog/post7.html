  • The gambit that deadlocked itself — abort-recall vs commit-recall, one log line ×11,206, the rescue Valkyrie never launched. (№1)
  • Fear ring > bravery ring — the runner geometrically could not reach the radius where the flag-dash begins; then it turned out it fled sightings at full health. (№6)
  • The triangle jiggle — two siege states, two destinations, two routes leaving opposite ways round an inlet; six minutes of zero-net walking. (№5)

Honesty box

The tournament's stuck-statistics came back inflated — by victory. After a match resolves the harness kept sampling the frozen battlefield, so every winner standing at the flag pedestal logged as "stuck" for the remaining ticks (the tell: pairs of equal-duration 600–800s episodes in resolved matches). The harness is patched; the numbers above are outcomes, which are artifact-free.

Where this leaves the war

Thirteen sweeps, one tournament, seven posts, twenty-four versions (v292→v315), three commits. Resolution went from "rare accident" to "the overwhelming norm," and the failures left are wars that ran out of army — which is a balance-and-rules conversation, not a debugging one. The lab now waits for its next story: unit promotions are on the drawing board, and every historical jotun-balance conclusion deserves a re-read now that the railguns are loaded.
