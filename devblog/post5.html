Lesson, permanently filed: when the owner's observations disagree with the harness, the harness is a suspect, not the owner.

Fix 1 — the triangle jiggle

Seed 11's sieger stood at one spot for six minutes with completely clear feelers. Per-second tracking caught why — two states, two destinations, two divergent routes:

t=495 assault → goal(−105,−120) route leaves WEST t=496 suppress → goal(−58,−166) route leaves EAST (around an inlet) t=499 assault → goal(−105,−120) …walk 20u back t=503 suppress → goal(−58,−166) …and again, for six minutes

Nothing was stuck — the journey kept being re-decided. At 100u out, suppress vs assault is a distinction without a difference: both mean "go siege that base." Fix: while the standoff is >70u away, suppress travels on the mission objective's route — identical to assault's — so state flaps can't thrash the trek. The radial standoff owns only the final approach.

Fix 2 — the pop-gun sieger

Why was a lurcher (reach 42u) sieging at all, with two jotuns (reach 80u) and a valkyrie in the garage? The substitution logic wanted a valkyrie, was saving the last one, and chose among abundant substitutes sorted by count: three lurchers beat two jotuns. The substitute pool's order already encodes role suitability (jotun first for siege work) — it now wins outright. Owning one extra lurcher is not a reason to send a pop-gun to do a railgun's job.
