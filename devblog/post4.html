The tank that fired blanks

2026-07-07 · v309–v311 · sweeps 7–9

Seed 25 had a 305-second block where two jotuns "fought" and nothing happened. This is the story of chasing it down through three layers, ending somewhere nobody expected: every jotun in every headless simulation ever run was firing blanks.

The clues

① Per-second data: both tanks parked at exactly 42u apart (their hold-and-pour range), hp pinned at 100% for 300s, ammo cycling 16→0→resupply→16→0. Every magazine, twice over, zero damage — both ways.
② The physics said reach was fine (slug: 115 speed × 0.7s = 80.5u). A ridge eating the rounds? The futility-escalation fix (v309) shipped for that… and the block reproduced tick-for-tick identically. Deterministic replay = the new code never ran = shotBlocked never fired = the rounds weren't hitting terrain either.
③ Direct probe at t=410: terrain between them flat, all land, clear line, blocked: 0. Then the slug tracker, set to record every projectile's closest approach… came back EMPTY. Not "the slugs missed" — there were no slugs.

The bug

if (idx === 3) setTimeout(discharge, 900);  // railgun charge

The railgun's 900ms charge delay was a real-time timer. A headless sweep runs the whole match inside one blocked JavaScript loop — real timers can never fire mid-loop. So every sim jotun shot: spent its ammo ✓, made its report sound ✓, set its cooldown ✓, discharged… nothing. No slug, no damage, no feedback. Live play was always fine (real timers work there) — which is exactly why no phone playtest ever caught it.

Blast radius: this didn't just freeze seed 25. Jotuns have dealt zero railgun damage in every tournament since the charge mechanic shipped — every stalemate rate and siege conclusion involving jotun combat was skewed against them, invisibly.

Fix (v310): charged shots queue on the game clock (real time live, sim clock headless) and tick from the shared projectile update — one code path, both worlds identical.

And with real railguns, a new king of flaps surfaced on seed 11: a sieging lurcher flipping suppress↔assault ×98 over 101s. Same LOS-strobe disease as the duel flap, new victim: the turret threat is only reported with a clear line, so strafing past wall edges blinks it once a second. Fix (v311): threat afterglow — a turret doesn't move, so a lost line holds the target for 2.5s (firing still requires genuine LOS).

⏳ Sweep 9 (v311) is running — threat-afterglow verification lands here.

Takeaway

The repetition detectors keep doing their job — but this one's lesson is about simulation fidelity: when a match replays tick-for-tick identically after a behavior change, the change didn't run, and that tell is as valuable as any graph. The sim harness is the AI's training ground; a physics lie there costs more than any single bad decision rule.
