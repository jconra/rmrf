VERDICT — 2026-07-19 smart siege standoff (ring-sampler + commit) — NOT SHIPPABLE YET
=====================================================================================

Jacob's design: to shell a tower, take the NEAREST one, sample a ring around it at radius = my
range (~10° steps), keep points that are on land + have a line to the tower + are NOT in any
other tower's range (no crossfire), nearest-to-me wins, then COMMIT (don't re-derive/hop every
tick — that hopping was the SW-corner route thrash; "the bridge spot would've been fine, why
didn't it keep it?"). Built as _siegeStandoff(v); disables the old radial spot + _standRot walk
while a smart stand owns the spot. Behind RR.setSmartStand / ?nosmartstand.

GATE (same build, ON default vs OFF=?nosmartstand, 30 deterministic seeds @1500s):

  resolved              29/30           30/30
  thrash-stuck samp     1751            1501 (-14%)   ← the pin thrash DID drop
  avg resolve           468s            572s (+22%)   ← SLOWER
  nav alarms            20              32 (+60%)     ← WORSE
  suppress GOTO viols   127             154           ← the tell

DIAGNOSIS — a reachability gap (mine, deferred at build time, and it bit):
  The sampler scores a spot on land + LOS-to-tower + no-crossfire, but NOT on whether the unit
  can actually A*-DRIVE there. So it commits to spots that are on-land with a clean shot but sit
  across water / behind a wall — unreachable. And because the smart path DISABLES the _standRot
  "walk to a reachable bearing" fallback, an unreachable committed stand just strands the unit on
  an order it can't fulfil → the surge in "suppress ordered to unreachable" violations, +alarms,
  slower matches. The COMMITMENT makes an unreachable pick STICK (worse than the old churn there).
  The design is sound (target sanity + commit + the thrash-stuck drop prove it); it's missing the
  4th test a human also applies: "...and can I get there?".

VERDICT: DO NOT SHIP as-is. NEXT: add reachability to the ring-sampler — a candidate only counts
as good if the unit can A*-route to it (planPath returns a real, non-partial route), and a
committed stand that goes unreachable is dropped + re-picked (next-nearest good spot). Re-gate;
expect alarms/resolve to come back to baseline-or-better with the thrash-stuck win kept. If
reachability-per-candidate is too costly per tick, gate it to the (rare) re-commit only, not the
steady state — the commit already makes steady-state cheap.
