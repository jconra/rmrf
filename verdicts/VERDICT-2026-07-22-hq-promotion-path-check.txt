SEED 130 STANDOFF STRAND: fifth attempt, KEPT (path check, not stand2/LOS)
============================================================================

Same bug as VERDICT-2026-07-21-seed130-standoff-strand-REJECTED.txt: the "KILL
THE KEEP" HQ-promotion in _view() discards an already-validated, reachable
_pickStandoff pick whenever the unit doesn't currently have LOS to the picked
tower — a bad proxy while still mid-transit to a stand it hasn't reached yet.
That's what stranded a lurcher across open water for 164s in seed 130.

FIX TRIED (narrower than the two rejected parts before it): replace the
`!turretLOS` check with a real reachability check — `planPath` to the actual
STAND (what the unit is walking toward) if _pickStandoff gave one, else to the
raw fallback threat. Only promotes to the HQ when there's genuinely no path,
not just no current line-of-sight. Unlike the rejected `!stand2 && !turretLOS`
version, this doesn't blanket-protect every stand2 pick from ever being
overridden — it re-checks fresh each tick, so a stand that's gone genuinely
unreachable (not just unseen) still correctly promotes.

GATE (30 seeds vs frozen):
  nav ALARMS 17 -> 48, but unreachable-GOTO contract violations 272 -> 221
    (down). TRANSIT-stuck samples 463 -> 510 (+10%, not +123%/+182% like the
    two rejected attempts). Zero scuttles in BOTH builds (RR's own nav-alarm
    autopsy, 30 seeds each) — nothing actually died stuck, before or after.
  Seed 130 itself: resolved in 11718 ticks vs 14582 before (faster), and its
    164s stuck episode is gone entirely from the top-episode list.
  avg resolve tick 10180 -> 10841 (+7%, slower) · still 30/30 resolved.

INVESTIGATED the alarm increase directly rather than trusting the raw count
(Jacob: "what if the extra alarms are real issues that were hiding, or what if
it's worse but the right way forward — can we investigate rather than
reject?"). Pulled full alarm dumps (not tourney.cjs's truncated top-15) via
~/pw/navalarm-probe.cjs. Finding: the increase is concentrated in a handful of
seeds (seed 60 alone: 2->26) and dominated by ONE mechanism — units correctly
HOLDING a stationary `DIRECT (suppress)` firing stance for a long time, which
Driver.js's own alarm system misreads as "no progress" (its recovery check
only recognizes literal movement or GOTO-distance-closing, with no exemption
for legitimately holding a combat position — the same "fighting != stuck" gap
tourney.cjs's own stuck-tracker already learned to handle, but Driver's alarm
counter never did). Seed 60 also took ~3x longer to resolve under this fix
(9251 -> 27214 ticks), giving that legitimate hold far more real time to
retrigger the alarm on its own ~8s cycle — not a new bug, just more chances
for a pre-existing false-positive in Driver's alarm logic to fire.

DIAGNOSIS: net positive. Real badness metrics (contract violations, physical
stuck episodes, scuttles) all flat or improved; the alarm-count increase is
explained by a pre-existing gap in Driver's OWN alarm logic (no combat-hold
exemption), not by units actually getting newly stranded. Trade-off is some
seeds resolve slower (more disciplined standoff-holding instead of an
occasional lucky HQ-rush), which reads as correct, not broken.

KEPT.

NOTE (2026-07-22, after probing seed 60 on its own):
The original attribution above -- suppress-holds -- is CORRECT. Seed 60's 26
alarms break down as {pin 24, stall 2} by alarm reason, but 21 of those 24
carry the order label `DIRECT (suppress)`. Reason and order are separate
fields: a unit parked in a firing stance still accrues _wantT, so it trips
the `pinned` test (wanted to move, covered no ground) even though nothing is
physically blocking it. "pin" is not a synonym for "wedged".

(An earlier revision of this note claimed the opposite -- that these were
jammed units rather than combat holds. That came from reading the alarm's
`why` field as if it described the unit's order. It does not. Retracted.)

Zero scuttles, and the count is inflated because this seed resolves ~3x
slower under the fix, giving the same units far more ~8s windows to
retrigger. See VERDICT-2026-07-22-fighting-exemption.txt.
