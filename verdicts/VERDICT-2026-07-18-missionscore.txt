VERDICT — 2026-07-18 MissionScore (the weighted mission picker replaces the playbook cascade)
==============================================================================================

Batch (uncommitted in /rmrf/): the commander's mission choice becomes ONE weight table —
a fight-or-flight for strategy (blueprint: devblog/post19.html; story: post20.html).
Dual-brain build: RR.setMissionWeights selects scorer vs classic cascade globally;
RR.setMissionWeightsTeam(team, on) runs them head-to-head in one match.

  - 13 scored missions: scout, attack, siege, siege-back (NEW rear-lane opener),
    capture ×4 DIRECTIONS (front/left/right/rear), defend, intercept, scavenge, sap, trap.
    The mission picks its own vehicle; what we own feeds the weight (fleet-comp:
    (type share − threshold) × 10 — FB→capture .40, Lurcher→attack .23,
    Valkyrie→siege-back .15, Jotun→siege .15).
  - SUCCESS MEMORY (the designer's north star: never repeat a failing mission): a failed
    tour drops that mission to −4, forgiven continuously (+1 per 8s). Directional captures
    each carry their OWN record — a runner dying on the front leaves the rear's clean.
  - FOG-HONEST LANE INTEL: a contact notebook fed only by what units actually saw/heard.
    Lanes score clear +1 (recent eyes, nothing known), blocked −2 (known contact on the
    corridor), unknown 0 — scouting EARNS capture confidence. No global-truth peeking.
  - CLOCK PRESSURE (the designer's rule): siege + capture ramp +1 per 5 min (cap +3) —
    late-game, somebody always forces a decision.
  - CRACK-THE-KEEP +4 (towers down, flag sealed): a dominant team goes and finishes.
  - GRADUAL WEIGHTS (house rule): terms scale with how true their condition is — early-game
    fades over 2 min, few-kills tapers across 3, just-lost stings then fades over 20s,
    losing scales with the fleet deficit (re-anchored to start at −2), success memory
    decays continuously. Facts (flag OPEN, need-parts) stay binary.
  - Anti-flap: +1.5 incumbent bonus + 8s re-think; DECISIVE score jumps (event spikes:
    flag stolen +12, flag loose +10, runner died, flag opened) re-decide immediately.
  - Every decision logs its full term math ("capture-right +11 (flag OPEN +4, lane clear
    +1, ...)") — RR.missionScores() feeds the ai-lab console.

THE CHAOS-SHINE LESSON: the 35–22 didn't survive re-measurement (30–29, 31–28 on
near-identical builds; the re-anchored mirror came out BYTE-IDENTICAL to the gradient
round while h2h still moved). Even a 60-match deterministic head-to-head carries ±4–5
games of build-to-build reshuffle. Determinism makes measurements exact, not comparisons
noise-free. One shiny scoreboard is not a mandate.
