DIAGNOSIS (not a fix — a characterized residual for a discuss-first combat change)
==================================================================================
2026-07-20: the KITE/engage grind (07-18 verdict's "~8 engage/KITE flaps" residual).

HARVEST (current build 85ae0c6, ~/pw/_kiteharvest.cjs, 30 seeds): 7 engage/KITE alarms, of
which ~2-3 are REAL oscillators (the rest recover after a beat). None cause a stalemate — every
one of these games resolves. So it's ~2-3/30 transient alarms, low frequency.

MECHANISM (from the recorder tails): an engage↔advance boundary strobe driven by seesEnemy
BLINKING (not distance — the 07-18 brawlR×1.4 already holds the distance boundary). Concrete:
  seed 102 blue lurcher @(25.7,50.2): ord cycles
     KITE → (15,-145) nose on threat (engage)   f=-1  (reverse toward tower cover)
     GOTO(4,101) advance to objective            f=+1  (drive to the mission goal)
  The two states pedal OPPOSITE (retreat-to-cover vs advance-to-goal) → net zero → grind. The
  enemy at (15,-145) is ~195u away (likely a Valkyrie, whose LOS over terrain blinks at range):
  each time it's visible → engage→KITE; each time it blinks out → seesEnemy false → engage drops
  → advance→GOTO. `pursuing` doesn't bridge the gap because it needs p.aggression>0.6, and these
  low-aggression Lurchers fall straight through to advance.

WHY IT'S NOT SHIPPED BLIND: the fix is a COMBAT-BEHAVIOR change, and Jacob's rules
([[feedback-discuss-before-code]], [[feedback-smarter-over-winrate]], [[feedback-ai-change-
tournament]]) say a combat change gets proposed + a before/after tournament (resolution delta,
not just alarms), not an autonomous ship. Two candidate fixes to weigh:

  (A) SIGHT-HOLD HYSTERESIS (mirror the shipped engage/suppress holds, one axis over): keep
      `engaging` true for ~0.8s after LOS drops if the last sighting is fresh + within a hold
      radius. Cost: _view must synthesize view.enemy from _lastEnemyPos during the hold (engage
      behavior dereferences view.enemy), so it's real surgery in _view, not a one-liner. Risk:
      a unit could keep firing at a ghost for the hold window.

  (B) ENGAGE-RANGE SANITY CAP (regardless of focus): don't KITE-retreat from an enemy far beyond
      your own weapon reach (the 195u case). Simpler, but it changes the focus-0 "fights on
      sight" semantics — a real aggression/balance shift that MUST be tournament-gated.

RECOMMENDATION: (A) is the principled one (it's the same anti-strobe philosophy as the two holds
shipped today), but both touch combat balance — hand to Jacob for the call + a before/after
tournament on whichever he picks. Tool: ~/pw/_kiteharvest.cjs (engage/KITE alarms + tails;
NOTE its flip counter is inflated by the GOTO progress-% in labels — read ord-TYPE changes, not
the raw count).

UPDATE 2026-07-21 — FIX (A) TRIED + REJECTED (net-negative). Implemented a KITE-specific afterglow
(cfg.kiteGlow=2.5 vs engageGlow 0.9): once a hurt unit commits to a retreat-to-cover, hold the
sighting through blinks so it doesn't drop to advance and drive back at the enemy. Clean A/B on
one build (RR.aiConfig('kiteGlow',0.9)=off vs 2.5=on), 30 seeds:
    resolved     30/30 → 30/30   (no change — already 30/30 from the siege hysteresis)
    nav alarms   28    → 34       (+6, WORSE)
    violations   265   → 265      (FLAT — advance issuer 79→72 but suppress 140→157: the hold
                                   didn't remove churn, it RELOCATED it from advance to suppress)
    avg resolve  562s  → 553s      (-2%, noise)
No clean grind falsification either (seed 102 slightly slower, 109 faster — chaos). Holding engage
longer just keeps a hurt unit in the combat states longer (lingering on a ghost), moving the churn
and adding pins. REVERTED to shipped state — a perturbation, not a fix, same as the shore try.
LEFT FOR LATER: fix (B) engage-range cap (don't KITE-retreat from an enemy far beyond weapon
reach — the 195u case), which attacks the ROOT (why engage a far enemy at all) rather than
papering over the blink; it shifts focus-0 "fights on sight" semantics, so it needs its own
before/after tournament with an eye on resolution, not just alarms.
