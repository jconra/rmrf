VERDICT — 2026-07-18 engage-distance hysteresis (the "terrain grind" that was really combat)
=============================================================================================

One-line change in AI.js (the `engaging` brain condition): once a unit is ALREADY in engage,
hold the brawl out to 1.4× brawlR instead of dropping it the instant a kited enemy drifts one
unit past the entry radius. Mirrors the fofStick bias that already anti-strobes the fight-SCORE
boundary; this anti-strobes the DISTANCE boundary.

THE HUNT (a forensics story, and two dead ends the instrument caught):
  "Terrain grinding" (the leftover driver alarms) was assumed to be nav — hulls sliding walls
  and shorelines. A geometry-fingerprint harvest (~/pw/grind-harvest.cjs: blocked-ring probe +
  water distance + recorder tail per alarm) split 49 alarms into "water edge" (26) and "open
  ground" (21). The open-ground class was misdiagnosed TWICE:
    1. FUSED HULLS — one scene showed a "friendly at 0.8u", so I built a hull-ahead governor +
       a separation nudge. Re-harvest came back BYTE-IDENTICAL; a separation counter read ZERO
       overlaps all match. The "0.8u friendly" was the SAME unit measured against its own stale
       alarm coordinate while it jittered in place. Both fixes reverted (no-ops).
    2. SHORE MARGIN vs GOVERNOR — I credited a water-class drop to a shore-margin A* cost, but
       never isolated it from the governor. Isolating it (shore-only) came back at 49 = fixing
       NOTHING. The alarm count is CHAOS-SENSITIVE: any perturbation reshuffles the deterministic
       matches ±10 alarms, and single-fix attribution is unreliable. All perturbation fixes
       reverted to clean HEAD.
  The one thing crisp through all of it: the flight recorder showed `ord` alternating
  KITE(engage) ↔ GOTO(advance) EVERY TICK — a boundary strobe at AI.js:213. The engage
  distance-gate had no hysteresis, so a kited enemy hovering at brawlR flipped the state each
  tick; the two states pull ~180° apart, the unit lurched in place, netted zero, and
  self-scuttled on a pin. That was the "open-ground grind" — combat, never terrain.

FALSIFICATION: seed 25 (a flap map) 6 alarms → 2; every diagnosed strobe position gone.

VERDICT: KEEP — a principled fix (kills a specific real oscillation), strictly better: same
resolution, faster, fewer alarms, no regression. The difference between a FIX and a
perturbation: a fix makes the diagnosed failure disappear reproducibly; a perturbation just
moves the chaos around.

RESIDUAL (the remaining 31, for later): ~8 still engage/KITE flaps (1.4× doesn't catch every
geometry — a wider or velocity-aware hold could); seed 95 is a pathological map (15 of the 31);

principled fixes with falsification, not count-chasing.
