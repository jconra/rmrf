VERDICT — siege standoff: out-of-reach release valve        2026-07-23

rmrf (candidate) vs ~/rmrf-frozen (baseline) · 30 seeds · full 480 map · det kit

PROBLEM (from the seed-60 dig + the 30-seed reachsurvey)
--------------------------------------------------------
A sieger in state=suppress can end up holding a target that sits BEYOND its own
SHOT_REACH: it cannot fire from there, and nothing in the loop ever revisits the
decision. Survey (pw/reachsurvey.cjs, 30 seeds): 74 such "held out of reach" stretches,
1554 unit-sec wasted, 48 of them never fired a shot. Seed 60 was the extreme — a dozen
consecutive lurchers each parked ~70u off a 42u gun, 27214-tick resolve vs ~10800 avg.
Two root causes identified:
  (1) DEAD BAND — nav arrives at slack 14u but the brain's atStand is 9u, so a unit can
      be "arrived" to nav and "not arrived" to the brain, parked in the 9-14u gap.
  (2) NO RELEASE — a genuinely unreachable stand is never given up.

CANDIDATES TESTED (each an independent knob)
--------------------------------------------
  A  baseline (frozen)
  B  both fixes on
  C  arrive-only  (aiStandArrive: nav drives to the firing spot at the brain's 9u tol)
  D  release-only (aiStandRelease v1: out-of-reach + not-firing for 12s -> drop stand, re-pick)
  D2 release-only REFINED (v1 + "not closing" gate: only release if range to target
     is also not shrinking — a unit still MARCHING in is out of range for the whole
     approach and must not be released)

VERDICT
-------
  arrive  : DROPPED (net harmful).
  release : SHIP. All three aggregate metrics improve, 22/30 untouched, net faster.
            Aligns with "smarter decisions beat win-rate": a unit no longer sits at full ammo
            outside its own range doing nothing.

  RESIDUAL RESOLVED (pw/releaseprobe.cjs, pw/winnerchk.cjs): seeds 74/179 regress ~+3-4k NOT
  from churn or a bad decision — it's chaotic divergence. The valve fires only 2 (seed 74) /
  3 (seed 179) times all match, each a correct release (unit genuinely idle out of its own
  range), and the SAME team still wins both (74 GREEN, 179 GREEN, on and off). One rare, right
  repositioning at t~360s ripples through the multi-agent sim into a longer — but still won —
  match. Suppressing those correct releases to shorten two specific seeds' clocks would be
  overfitting. The aggregate (5 faster / 3 slower, net -8518, same winners) is the real signal.
